# Market Competitive Research

## What this means

The team has surveyed the competitive and market landscape to understand how analogous problems are solved by other products, platforms, or industry peers. This optional research informs product positioning, design decisions, and feature prioritization by grounding the initiative in external context.

## Why it matters

Understanding how competitors and industry leaders approach similar problems prevents reinventing the wheel, surfaces proven patterns worth adopting, and highlights differentiation opportunities. It also helps justify build-vs-buy decisions and informs realistic user expectations.

## How to complete this

- Identify direct and indirect competitors or comparable products addressing the same user need
- Document key design and implementation approaches observed in the market
- Evaluate open-source solutions, third-party libraries, or SaaS tools that could reduce build scope
- Note differentiators — areas where the initiative should outperform or diverge from existing solutions
- Summarize findings with actionable implications for design and technical decisions

## Definition of done

- A structured competitive analysis document exists with at least three relevant comparators
- Build-vs-buy recommendations are documented where applicable
- Key insights are reflected in product or architecture decisions
- The research is stored and linked from the initiative's planning artifacts
