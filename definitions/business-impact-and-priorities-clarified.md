# Business Impact and Priorities Clarified

## What this means
This item ensures the team understands how this initiative connects to business objectives, what its relative priority is compared to other work, and what the cost of inaction would be. It also clarifies who is sponsoring the work and what trade-offs are acceptable.

## Why it matters
Engineering teams that don't understand business context make poor trade-off decisions. Understanding that a feature is a "must ship for enterprise deal" vs. "nice to have for Q3" fundamentally changes how teams approach scope, quality, and timeline. Unclear priorities lead to over-investing in low-impact work and under-resourcing what matters most.

## How to complete this
- Document how this initiative maps to company or team OKRs
- Quantify business impact where possible (revenue, retention, cost reduction, compliance risk)
- Clarify priority relative to other concurrent initiatives — is this blocking anything?
- Identify the executive or business sponsor who is accountable for the outcome
- Document the cost or risk of NOT doing this (helps with trade-off conversations)

## Definition of done
- [ ] Business impact is quantified or clearly described
- [ ] Priority level documented relative to other active initiatives
- [ ] Executive or business sponsor identified
- [ ] Cost of inaction documented
