# External Team Coordination Planned

## What this means
External team coordination covers the inter-team communication, synchronization, and handoffs required when multiple teams need to deliver changes in concert. This includes planning joint sprint cycles, API review meetings, coordinated deployments, and shared testing environments.

## Why it matters
Cross-team dependencies are among the most common causes of launch delays. When teams discover they need something from another team at the wrong time — mid-sprint, during a release freeze, or after the other team's cycle has already been planned — everyone scrambles. Proactive coordination planning ensures that dependent work is sequenced correctly and each team knows what they need to deliver and when.

## How to complete this
- Identify all teams whose work is required for this initiative to succeed
- For each dependency, define: what is needed, by when, and who is responsible
- Schedule coordination touchpoints early — joint planning sessions, API review meetings, integration testing windows
- Align on deployment sequencing — who deploys first? What's the coordination plan for go-live?
- Establish a shared communication channel for cross-team coordination
- Track cross-team commitments as formally as internal ones

## Definition of done
- [ ] All external team dependencies identified with timing requirements
- [ ] Coordination touchpoints scheduled or committed to
- [ ] Deployment sequencing plan documented
- [ ] Shared communication channel established
- [ ] Cross-team commitments confirmed with respective team leads
