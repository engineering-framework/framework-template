# Budget Requirements Identified

## What this means
Budget requirements identify all monetary costs associated with this initiative — infrastructure, software licenses, third-party services, external development, and ongoing operational costs. The goal is to ensure the business understands the full financial picture before committing to the initiative.

## Why it matters
Engineering initiatives have real costs that are often invisible to business stakeholders until they appear on a cloud bill or a procurement request. Identifying budget requirements upfront allows finance to plan, prevents surprise budget requests mid-project, and gives leadership the information needed to make informed investment decisions. Undisclosed costs damage trust with finance and leadership.

## How to complete this
- Use the Budget Requirements template to itemize all one-time and recurring costs
- Include: infrastructure (compute, storage, database), software licenses, third-party API costs, external development if applicable
- Estimate costs at current scale and at projected scale (12 months)
- Identify which costs require procurement approval vs. can be expensed directly
- Get approval for any new vendors or contracts before committing to approaches that depend on them

## Definition of done
- [ ] All one-time costs documented
- [ ] Recurring costs documented with monthly/annual estimates
- [ ] Budget submitted for approval with appropriate lead time
- [ ] No budget dependencies (vendor contracts, licenses) assumed to be in place without confirmation
