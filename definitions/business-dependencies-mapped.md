# Business Dependencies Mapped

## What this means
Business dependencies are non-technical blockers or prerequisites — processes, decisions, approvals, partnerships, contracts, or organizational changes that must happen before or alongside the technical work. This is distinct from technical dependencies, which are tracked separately.

## Why it matters
Many engineering initiatives stall not because of technical challenges, but because of unmapped business dependencies: a legal review that wasn't started early enough, a vendor contract that needs 60-day lead time, an organizational change that hasn't been communicated. Mapping these early ensures the critical path includes business activities, not just engineering milestones.

## How to complete this
- Identify all external approvals required (legal, compliance, security, finance, leadership)
- Map partner or vendor dependencies: are contracts in place? Do SLAs need to be negotiated?
- Identify cross-functional activities that must run in parallel (e.g., marketing prep, sales enablement, support training)
- Document the lead time for each dependency so they can be started at the right time
- Assign owners for each business dependency outside the engineering team

## Definition of done
- [ ] All non-technical dependencies identified and listed
- [ ] Lead times estimated for each dependency
- [ ] Owners assigned to each dependency outside the engineering team
- [ ] Critical path updated to include dependencies with long lead times
