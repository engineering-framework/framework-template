# Regulatory Requirements Reviewed

## What this means
A regulatory requirements review confirms that all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards have been reviewed in the context of this specific initiative. This is a broader check than compliance requirements identification — it's the sign-off that nothing has been missed and the team has the clarity needed to proceed.

## Why it matters
Regulatory requirements change, vary by geography, and are sometimes discovered late (especially as organizations expand into new markets or customer segments). A formal review step creates accountability that regulations have been considered, documented, and addressed — not assumed to be handled by someone else or covered by existing controls.

## How to complete this
- Review the list of applicable regulations identified in the compliance requirements step
- Confirm applicability to this specific initiative (some regulations only apply under certain conditions, data types, or user populations)
- Check for any recent regulatory changes that might affect the implementation
- Engage legal counsel for any areas of ambiguity or high regulatory risk
- Document the review outcome and any open questions or obligations
- Obtain legal or compliance team sign-off for initiatives in highly regulated domains

## Definition of done
- [ ] Applicable regulations reviewed and confirmed relevant or not relevant to this initiative
- [ ] Recent regulatory changes checked
- [ ] Ambiguous areas escalated to legal counsel
- [ ] Review documented with sign-off from appropriate stakeholder
