# Decision-making Authority Established

## What this means

The team has explicitly defined who holds decision-making authority for different categories of choices across the initiative — technical architecture, product scope, resource allocation, and release decisions. This clarity prevents decisions from stalling in consensus loops or being made by the wrong people.

## Why it matters

Ambiguous decision authority is one of the most common causes of initiative delays. When it is unclear who can approve a technical approach or scope change, teams default to escalation chains that slow velocity and erode trust. Explicit authority structures enable fast, confident decisions at the right level.

## How to complete this

- Categorize the types of decisions the initiative will face: technical, product, security, resourcing, and release
- Assign a decision owner (single DRI) for each category, not a committee
- Define which decisions require consultation vs. consensus vs. notification only
- Document the authority structure in a shared location visible to all initiative participants
- Review and validate the authority map with leadership before the initiative enters active development

## Definition of done

- A decision authority document or RACI matrix is created and stored with initiative artifacts
- Each major decision category has a named DRI identified
- The authority structure has been communicated to and accepted by all initiative leads
- Escalation triggers — when a decision should move up the chain — are explicitly documented
