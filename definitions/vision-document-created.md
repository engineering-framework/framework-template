# Vision Document Created

## What this means

A vision document articulates the future state the initiative is working toward — the user experience, business outcome, and technical landscape that will exist after successful delivery. It provides a north star that guides day-to-day decisions and helps the team evaluate trade-offs against a shared picture of success.

## Why it matters

Teams that lack a documented vision drift toward locally optimal decisions that conflict at the system level. The vision document ensures that product, engineering, and design are aligned on the destination, reducing rework caused by divergent interpretations of requirements and scope.

## How to complete this

- Describe the target user experience in concrete terms — what users will be able to do and feel
- Articulate the business outcome: measurable value the initiative delivers to the organization
- Describe the technical future state at a high level, including major architectural shifts
- State explicitly what is out of scope for this initiative to prevent scope creep
- Socialize the draft with key stakeholders and iterate until there is genuine alignment

## Definition of done

- A vision document is authored and stored in the initiative's knowledge base
- The document covers user experience, business outcomes, and out-of-scope boundaries
- It has been reviewed and signed off by product, engineering, and design leads
- The vision is referenced in the initiative kickoff and planning artifacts
