# Go-to-market Coordination Completed

## What this means
Go-to-market (GTM) coordination ensures that the business and customer-facing sides of the launch are aligned with the engineering delivery. This includes marketing announcements, customer notifications, sales enablement, pricing decisions, and documentation — all timed to coincide with the technical launch.

## Why it matters
A technically perfect launch that no one knows about delivers no business value. Conversely, a marketing announcement that precedes a delayed technical launch creates customer frustration and support overhead. GTM coordination ensures the technical and business launches happen in sync, and that all customer-facing teams are prepared.

## How to complete this
- Identify all GTM activities associated with this launch (blog post, email announcement, in-app messaging, press release, product hunt, etc.)
- Coordinate with marketing on launch timing and messaging
- Brief the sales team on the new capability with demo materials and competitive positioning
- Update customer-facing documentation before launch
- Define the customer notification plan: who gets notified, how, and when?
- Confirm feature availability aligns with any previously made public commitments

## Definition of done
- [ ] GTM activities identified and owners assigned
- [ ] Marketing timeline aligned with engineering launch date
- [ ] Sales team briefed with demo materials
- [ ] Customer-facing documentation updated
- [ ] Customer notification plan confirmed
