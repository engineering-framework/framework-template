# Risk Mitigation Strategies Defined

## What this means
Risk mitigation strategies are concrete actions that reduce either the probability of a risk occurring or its impact if it does. This item ensures that every risk identified in the risk register has a corresponding mitigation plan — not just an acknowledgment that the risk exists.

## Why it matters
A risk register full of risks with no mitigations is just an anxiety list. The value of risk identification is in driving action: de-risking spikes, adding buffer, building fallback implementations, obtaining approvals early. Defined mitigation strategies turn risk identification from a documentation exercise into a project management tool that actually changes how the project is executed.

## How to complete this
- Review the risk register and ensure every risk scoring ≥ 6 has a documented mitigation strategy
- Classify mitigations: avoid (change the approach), reduce (implement controls), transfer (e.g., insurance, contract), or accept (document the risk and monitor)
- For each mitigation, assign an owner and a target date
- Track mitigation execution — a strategy that isn't executed is no better than none
- Re-evaluate risk scores after mitigations are in place

## Definition of done
- [ ] Every risk scoring ≥ 6 has a documented mitigation strategy
- [ ] Mitigation strategies have owners and target dates
- [ ] Mitigations are being tracked to completion
- [ ] Risk register updated after mitigations are applied
