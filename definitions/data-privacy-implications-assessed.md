# Data Privacy Implications Assessed

## What this means
Data privacy assessment evaluates how this initiative handles personal data — what PII is collected or processed, what the legal basis for processing is, how data is stored and protected, how long it's retained, and how user rights (access, deletion, portability) are supported.

## Why it matters
Privacy is not just a compliance requirement — it's increasingly a product expectation. Regulations like GDPR, CCPA, and PIPL impose significant obligations and penalties for mishandling personal data. More importantly, breaches of user trust through poor privacy practices cause lasting reputational damage. Assessing privacy implications before building ensures the right controls are designed in from the start.

## How to complete this
- Identify all personal data this initiative will collect, store, or process (names, emails, IP addresses, behavioral data, etc.)
- Determine the legal basis for each type of processing under applicable privacy law
- Evaluate data minimization: are you collecting only what's necessary?
- Design data retention and deletion policies — when is data deleted, and how is deletion propagated?
- Ensure user rights workflows exist (access requests, deletion requests, data portability)
- Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) if required by your compliance framework

## Definition of done
- [ ] All PII collected or processed is identified and documented
- [ ] Legal basis for processing documented for each category of personal data
- [ ] Retention and deletion policies defined
- [ ] Privacy implications reviewed with legal or DPO
