# Regular Status Update Cadence Established

## What this means
A status update cadence is a committed, recurring schedule for communicating the initiative's progress to stakeholders — covering what was accomplished, what's coming next, what's blocked, and whether the timeline is on track. It replaces "updates on demand" with predictable, proactive communication.

## Why it matters
Stakeholders who don't receive regular updates fill the information gap by asking questions, scheduling ad-hoc meetings, or making up their own narratives about what's happening. Predictable updates are more efficient for both the team and stakeholders: they reduce interruption, build trust, and surface problems early when there's still time to course-correct.

## How to complete this
- Agree on the update frequency with stakeholders (weekly for most initiatives; bi-weekly for lower-stakes work)
- Define the format: async written update, live sync, or dashboard
- Choose the communication channel (Slack, email, Confluence, project tracker)
- Assign ownership — who writes the update and by when?
- Define what the update covers: progress, upcoming milestones, risks/blockers, asks from stakeholders
- Commit to the schedule before the project starts, not as an afterthought

## Definition of done
- [ ] Update cadence agreed upon with stakeholders
- [ ] Format and channel defined
- [ ] Owner assigned for producing updates
- [ ] First update scheduled (don't wait for something to report — send a kickoff update)
