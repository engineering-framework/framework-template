# Resource Allocation Planned

## What this means
Resource allocation planning assigns specific team members to specific work streams, balancing specialization with knowledge-sharing, and ensures that no individual is over-committed or becomes a single point of failure. It maps people to work, not just capacity to scope.

## Why it matters
Unplanned resource allocation leads to all the critical work ending up on the same one or two people (often the most senior engineers), creating bottlenecks, burnout risk, and bus-factor exposure. Explicit allocation enables parallel work streams, ensures the right expertise is applied where it's needed, and identifies when additional resources need to be brought in before it becomes a crisis.

## How to complete this
- Map work streams to team members based on skills and availability
- Identify single points of failure: components where only one person has the knowledge or ability to deliver
- Plan for knowledge transfer and pairing on critical components to reduce bus-factor
- Confirm that the allocation doesn't create hidden overloads (engineers committed to multiple priorities simultaneously)
- Adjust scope or timeline if the required allocation is not achievable with the current team

## Definition of done
- [ ] Work streams assigned to specific engineers
- [ ] Single points of failure identified and mitigated
- [ ] No engineer allocated beyond their capacity
- [ ] Allocation reviewed by engineering manager
