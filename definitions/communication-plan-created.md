# Communication Plan Created

## What this means
The communication plan documents how information about this initiative will flow to all stakeholders — what gets communicated, to whom, via what channel, at what frequency, and who is responsible. It covers internal team communication, cross-functional stakeholders, and external customer-facing communications.

## Why it matters
Communication gaps create the conditions for stakeholders to be surprised by delays, scope changes, or launch events. Surprises erode trust and create reactive scrambles. A communication plan ensures that everyone who needs to be informed, is informed — at the right time, with the right level of detail for their role, without requiring anyone to chase information.

## How to complete this
- Map all stakeholders and their communication needs (use the Communication Plan template)
- Define the update cadence for each stakeholder group
- Assign ownership for each communication touchpoint
- Plan launch communications in advance: announcements, release notes, internal briefings, customer notifications
- Define escalation communications: how and when will issues be escalated up the chain?
