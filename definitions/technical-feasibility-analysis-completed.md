# Technical Feasibility Analysis Completed

## What this means

The engineering team has evaluated whether the proposed solution is technically achievable within the known constraints of the existing system, team capabilities, and organizational timelines. This analysis produces a documented assessment — not just a verbal gut check — covering technology choices, integration complexity, and known unknowns.

## Why it matters

Starting implementation without validating feasibility is a primary driver of late-stage redesigns and missed commitments. A completed feasibility analysis surfaces blockers early, informs scope decisions, and gives leadership accurate signal on delivery confidence before significant investment is made.

## How to complete this

- Assess compatibility of proposed approaches with existing system architecture and technology stack
- Identify technical unknowns and spikes required to reduce uncertainty in key risk areas
- Evaluate team skills against the technical demands of the initiative and flag gaps
- Estimate rough implementation complexity and flag areas where assumptions are fragile
- Document findings with a clear recommendation: proceed, proceed with caveats, or reassess scope

## Definition of done

- A written feasibility assessment exists and is linked from the initiative planning document
- All major technical unknowns are identified and assigned for investigation
- The assessment includes a clear overall feasibility verdict with supporting rationale
- Engineering leadership has reviewed and acknowledged the findings
