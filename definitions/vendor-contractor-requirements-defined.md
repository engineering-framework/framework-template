# Vendor / Contractor Requirements Defined

## What this means
Vendor and contractor requirements formally specify what an external party must deliver, how success will be measured, what the contractual terms are, and how the engagement will be managed. This goes beyond identifying that external help is needed — it defines the detailed requirements that make procurement and engagement management successful.

## Why it matters
Vague vendor requirements lead to misaligned expectations, scope disputes, and unsatisfactory deliverables. Clear requirements give vendors the information they need to provide accurate proposals, protect the organization in contract negotiations, and create the foundation for holding vendors accountable to their commitments. They also make evaluation objective: multiple vendors can be compared against the same criteria.

## How to complete this
- Use the Vendor/Contractor Requirements template to document the full scope of work
- Define deliverables precisely with acceptance criteria — what does "done" look like for each deliverable?
- Specify security and compliance requirements that the vendor must meet
- Define IP ownership, confidentiality requirements, and contract terms needed
- Establish the evaluation criteria and scoring methodology before reviewing proposals
- Include a handoff plan: how will knowledge be transferred to the internal team?

## Definition of done
- [ ] Requirements documented using the standard template
- [ ] Deliverables defined with acceptance criteria
- [ ] Security and compliance requirements specified
- [ ] Contract terms requirements defined and reviewed by legal
- [ ] Evaluation criteria documented
