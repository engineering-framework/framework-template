# Cross-functional Team Composition Defined

## What this means

The full set of roles and individuals required to deliver the initiative has been identified and documented. This includes engineering, product, design, QA, security, data, operations, and any other functions whose involvement is necessary for successful delivery.

## Why it matters

Incomplete team composition leads to late discovery of resource gaps, hand-off delays, and work that must be redone when missing disciplines are brought in mid-stream. Defining the team composition upfront enables realistic capacity planning and ensures all perspectives are present during critical design decisions.

## How to complete this

- Map all functional areas required: engineering squads, product management, design, QA, security, legal, ops, data
- Identify named individuals or at minimum responsible teams for each function
- Clarify part-time vs. full-time involvement and expected contribution periods
- Document the team composition in the initiative planning artifact with clear ownership per area
- Confirm availability and alignment with each function's leadership

## Definition of done

- A team roster or RACI is documented and accessible to all initiative participants
- All required functional areas are represented with named owners or team assignments
- Part-time commitments are explicitly noted with expected availability windows
- Team composition has been reviewed by the initiative lead and relevant department heads
