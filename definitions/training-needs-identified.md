# Training Needs Identified

## What this means
Training needs assessment identifies which teams or individuals need training or enablement before or after this initiative ships — support teams learning new features to handle tickets, sales teams needing product demos to sell the new capability, or engineers needing onboarding to new technologies introduced by the initiative.

## Why it matters
Features that ship without adequate enablement of the people who support and sell them create post-launch chaos. Support teams get flooded with tickets they can't answer. Sales teams can't demo the product confidently. Internal engineers can't maintain the system because no one documented how it works. Identifying training needs early creates time to build the materials and schedule the sessions.

## How to complete this
- Identify all teams who will interact with this initiative post-launch (support, sales, CS, marketing, engineering)
- Assess each team's current knowledge gap relative to what they'll need
- Determine the training format: written documentation, recorded demo, live session, or hands-on workshop
- Create a training plan with owners, materials, and delivery schedule
- Ensure training is delivered before launch, not after

## Definition of done
- [ ] All teams requiring training identified
- [ ] Knowledge gaps assessed per team
- [ ] Training plan created with materials, owners, and delivery dates
- [ ] Training confirmed to be delivered before launch
