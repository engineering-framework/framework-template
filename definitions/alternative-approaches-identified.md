# Alternative Approaches Identified

## What this means
In the context of contingency planning, alternative approaches are the backup strategies available if the primary plan fails or becomes blocked. These are not design alternatives considered and rejected during planning — they're the "Plan B" that the team can execute if the primary approach encounters an insurmountable obstacle.

## Why it matters
Projects that have no alternative approaches are binary: either the primary plan works, or the initiative fails. Teams that identify alternatives in advance can pivot quickly when they hit a wall, rather than spending weeks in limbo. Having a documented Plan B also gives stakeholders confidence that the team has thought through failure scenarios.

## How to complete this
- For each major risk or uncertain component, identify at least one alternative approach if the primary fails
- Assess the trade-offs of each alternative: what's gained, what's lost compared to the primary?
- Define the conditions under which you would switch to the alternative (trigger criteria)
- Document what preparatory work, if any, would make the alternative faster to execute if needed
- Ensure the team is aware of the alternatives and empowered to propose switching if they see early warning signs

## Definition of done
- [ ] Alternative approaches identified for high-risk components
- [ ] Trade-offs and trigger conditions documented
- [ ] Alternatives reviewed and agreed upon by tech lead and PM
