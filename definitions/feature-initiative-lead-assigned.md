# Feature Initiative Lead Assigned

## What this means

A single accountable engineering lead has been formally designated for the initiative. This person owns technical direction, coordinates cross-functional work, drives decisions to closure, and serves as the primary point of escalation for engineering concerns throughout the initiative's lifecycle.

## Why it matters

Initiatives without a clear technical lead suffer from diffused accountability, slower decision-making, and coordination gaps that compound over time. Assigning a lead early ensures someone is actively monitoring risks, maintaining momentum, and representing engineering interests in cross-functional planning.

## How to complete this

- Identify a senior engineer with sufficient context, capacity, and authority for the initiative scope
- Confirm the lead assignment with the individual, their manager, and the product counterpart
- Define the lead's responsibilities: design oversight, decision authority, cross-team coordination, and status reporting
- Ensure the lead has access to all relevant planning artifacts, stakeholders, and communication channels
- Communicate the assignment broadly so all involved parties know who to engage

## Definition of done

- A named initiative lead is documented in the initiative's primary planning artifact
- The lead has accepted the role and has been briefed on scope and expectations
- Engineering and product leadership have confirmed the assignment
- The lead's responsibilities are explicitly defined and understood
