# Timeline Constraints Identified

## What this means
Timeline constraints are hard or soft deadlines that exist independent of the team's capacity — contractual commitments, regulatory deadlines, conference announcements, fiscal year boundaries, or dependencies on other teams' launches. Identifying these early allows the team to plan realistically rather than discovering constraints mid-execution.

## Why it matters
Discovering a hard deadline late in a project forces scope cuts under pressure, reducing quality and increasing risk. When constraints are surfaced early, the team can negotiate scope, add resources, or push back on unrealistic timelines before commitments are made. Teams that operate without understanding timeline constraints routinely overpromise and underdeliver.

## How to complete this
- Ask the business sponsor and PM: "Are there any hard deadlines we must hit and why?"
- Check for contractual commitments to customers that depend on this feature
- Identify dependencies that block other teams — what are their timelines?
- Document external events (conferences, fiscal quarters, regulatory enforcement dates) that create soft deadlines
- Distinguish between hard constraints (missing = significant consequence) and soft preferences

## Definition of done
- [ ] All known hard deadlines documented with reasons
- [ ] Soft deadline preferences documented
- [ ] Dependencies on other teams' timelines identified
- [ ] Team has validated that the constraints are achievable given current scope and resources, or trade-offs have been proposed
