# Compliance Requirements Identified

## What this means
Compliance requirements are the legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations that apply to this initiative. These may come from industry regulations (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOX), organizational policies, customer contracts, or certification frameworks (SOC 2, ISO 27001). Identifying them ensures the system is built to meet these obligations from the start.

## Why it matters
Compliance gaps discovered after launch are expensive to remediate and can result in fines, lost business, or legal liability. Many compliance requirements have technical implications that affect architecture, data retention, audit logging, and encryption — decisions that are costly to change post-launch. Identifying requirements early ensures they're factored into the design.

## How to complete this
- Consult with legal and compliance teams to identify all applicable regulatory frameworks
- Review customer contracts for security and compliance clauses that apply to this initiative
- Identify any existing compliance certifications the organization holds and whether this initiative falls under their scope
- Translate compliance obligations into specific technical requirements (e.g., "GDPR Article 30 requires maintaining records of processing activities")
- Document any compliance requirements that need a formal review or sign-off before launch

## Definition of done
- [ ] All applicable regulatory frameworks identified
- [ ] Compliance obligations translated into technical requirements
- [ ] Legal or compliance team consulted and requirements confirmed
- [ ] Compliance requirements included in the development scope
