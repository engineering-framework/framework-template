# Success Criteria and KPIs Defined

## What this means
Success criteria and KPIs establish the measurable outcomes that define whether this initiative has achieved its goals. This goes beyond vague statements like "improve performance" to specify exact metrics, baseline values, and targets with timelines.

## Why it matters
Without measurable success criteria, teams have no objective way to evaluate outcomes, and stakeholders can't agree on whether to continue investing or change direction. Poorly defined success metrics lead to initiatives that feel complete but don't actually solve the problem, or worse — teams optimizing for the wrong thing.

## How to complete this
- Identify 2–4 primary metrics that directly measure the problem you're solving (not just output metrics)
- Measure and document current baseline values before making any changes
- Set specific, time-bound targets (e.g., "reduce p99 API latency from 800ms to under 200ms by launch + 30 days")
- Define secondary/leading indicators to detect problems early
- Document the measurement methodology: what tool, what query, what time window
- Call out anti-metrics — things you explicitly will not sacrifice to hit the primary metrics

## Definition of done
- [ ] 2+ primary success metrics defined with documented baselines and targets
- [ ] Measurement methodology documented (tool, query, cadence)
- [ ] Success criteria reviewed and approved by PM and engineering lead
- [ ] Dashboard or reporting mechanism in place to track metrics post-launch
