# Technical Risks Identified and Documented

## What this means
Technical risks are implementation challenges, architectural uncertainties, or technology choices that could prevent the initiative from being delivered on time, at the expected quality, or with the expected performance characteristics. Identifying them early creates time to spike, prototype, or plan mitigations.

## Why it matters
Unknown risks are the ones that derail projects. When teams identify technical risks explicitly and early, they can invest in de-risking activities (proof-of-concept, prototyping, early integration testing) before the risk materializes mid-sprint as an unexpected blocker. Documented risks also create transparency with stakeholders who may not realize the engineering complexity involved.

## How to complete this
- Conduct a risk brainstorm with the engineering team: "What could go wrong technically?"
- For each risk, assess likelihood and impact (use the Risk Register template)
- Prioritize risks with a score of 10+ for active mitigation planning
- For high-likelihood technical risks, schedule a time-boxed spike to validate the approach early
- Document all identified risks, even low-likelihood ones, so they're visible

## Definition of done
- [ ] Risk brainstorm completed with the engineering team
- [ ] All technical risks documented in the risk register
- [ ] Likelihood and impact scored for each risk
- [ ] Mitigation plans created for risks scoring ≥ 10
- [ ] Spikes or proof-of-concepts scheduled for highest-risk unknowns
