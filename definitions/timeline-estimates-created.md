# Timeline Estimates Created

## What this means
Timeline estimates translate the project scope into a realistic delivery schedule — mapping stories and tasks to calendar time, accounting for team capacity, dependencies, and risk. This produces a schedule the team believes in rather than one handed down from above.

## Why it matters
Estimates created by the engineers doing the work are significantly more accurate than top-down estimates. Bottom-up estimation also creates ownership: teams that estimate their own work feel accountable for hitting it. Timeline estimates also enable early identification of impossible commitments — a project that takes 16 weeks cannot be delivered in 6 weeks, and the sooner that's known, the better.

## How to complete this
- Break the scope into estimatable units (stories, tasks, or components)
- Estimate each unit using story points, days, or t-shirt sizing — choose the method your team trusts
- Sum estimates and compare to available capacity to produce a delivery date range
- Express estimates as ranges (not single points) and communicate confidence levels
- Include time for QA, documentation, and deployment — not just implementation
- Present the estimate with explicit assumptions so it can be validated or challenged

## Definition of done
- [ ] All stories/tasks estimated by the engineers who will implement them
- [ ] Total estimate compared to available capacity
- [ ] Delivery date range (not single point) communicated to stakeholders
- [ ] Assumptions behind the estimate documented
- [ ] Estimate reviewed and accepted by tech lead and PM
