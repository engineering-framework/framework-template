# Escalation Paths Defined

## What this means

The team has documented explicit escalation paths for the scenarios most likely to block progress — unresolved technical disagreements, cross-team dependency conflicts, scope disputes, resource gaps, and security or compliance concerns. Each path identifies who to engage and at what threshold.

## Why it matters

Without predefined escalation paths, teams burn time determining who has authority to resolve a blocker. Clear escalation paths reduce the cost of conflict resolution, ensure issues reach the right people quickly, and prevent small blockers from growing into initiative-threatening delays.

## How to complete this

- Identify the most probable escalation scenarios: technical blockers, cross-team conflicts, scope disagreements, and resource disputes
- For each scenario, define the escalation trigger, first point of contact, and secondary escalation if needed
- Confirm named individuals are aware they are on the escalation path and agree to the role
- Document response time expectations for each escalation level
- Communicate the escalation paths to all initiative participants at kickoff

## Definition of done

- An escalation path document is created covering at least four distinct scenario types
- Named escalation contacts have been confirmed and are aware of their role
- The document is accessible to all initiative participants
- Escalation paths are reviewed and approved by initiative leadership
