# QA Team Engaged and Requirements Shared

## What this means
Engaging the QA team early means bringing them into the design process — not just handing them completed code to test. This involves sharing requirements, user stories, and technical designs with QA so they can plan their testing approach, identify testability concerns, and begin writing test plans in parallel with development.

## Why it matters
QA teams that are only engaged at the end of development face constant pressure to compress testing. The result is rushed tests, missed edge cases, and quality issues discovered in production. When QA is engaged early, they can flag design decisions that make testing hard, ensure testability is considered in the API design, and have test plans ready the moment development completes.

## How to complete this
- Schedule a kickoff meeting with the QA team to walk through requirements and user stories
- Share all acceptance criteria in a format QA can use to derive test cases
- Clarify any ambiguities in requirements together — often QA asks the best clarifying questions
- Discuss the testing environment, test data requirements, and what automation is feasible
- Establish the quality gate criteria: what does "ready for QA" and "QA complete" mean for this initiative?

## Definition of done
- [ ] QA kickoff meeting completed
- [ ] All requirements, user stories, and acceptance criteria shared with QA
- [ ] Test data and environment requirements documented
- [ ] Quality gate criteria agreed upon
- [ ] QA team has started test planning in parallel with development
