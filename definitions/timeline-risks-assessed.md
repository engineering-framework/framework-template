# Timeline Risks Assessed

## What this means
Timeline risks are factors that could cause the initiative to slip beyond its target dates — scope creep, underestimated complexity, external dependencies, team availability changes, or coordination failures. Assessing these risks means identifying them explicitly and having contingency plans.

## Why it matters
Most software projects are late because timeline risks were identified but not acted upon. The risks were visible — in retrospect, someone always knew — but weren't formally tracked or communicated. A formal assessment creates accountability and prompts the team to either address the risks proactively or set realistic expectations with stakeholders before the slip happens, not after.

## How to complete this
- Review the project plan with fresh eyes, looking for optimistic assumptions
- Identify all external dependencies that have their own timelines outside your control
- Assess the team's historical velocity and compare to the current estimate
- Identify potential scope creep vectors and define a change management process
- Build buffer into the timeline proportional to the complexity and number of unknowns
- Communicate timeline risks to stakeholders early, with options (descope, extend, add resources)

## Definition of done
- [ ] Timeline risks identified and documented in the risk register
- [ ] Buffer built into the project timeline proportional to uncertainty
- [ ] External dependencies with their own timelines identified and tracked
- [ ] Stakeholders informed of timeline risks with options presented
