# Problem Space Research Documented

## What this means

The team has thoroughly investigated and documented the problem domain before committing to a solution. This includes understanding user pain points, existing workflows, and the root causes of the problem being addressed — not just its surface symptoms.

## Why it matters

Building the wrong thing is the most expensive mistake an engineering team can make. Rigorous problem space research ensures the initiative addresses a real, validated need and that the proposed solution targets root causes rather than symptoms. It also creates a shared understanding across engineering, product, and stakeholders.

## How to complete this

- Conduct user and stakeholder interviews to gather qualitative data on pain points and current workarounds
- Analyze usage data, support tickets, and feedback channels to identify quantitative evidence of the problem
- Map current-state workflows and identify friction points, inefficiencies, or failure modes
- Document assumptions explicitly and mark which have been validated vs. still require validation
- Synthesize findings into a structured research summary that is accessible to the full team

## Definition of done

- A research document is authored, reviewed, and stored in the team's knowledge base
- Key findings are summarized and linked from the initiative's primary planning artifact
- At least two distinct sources of evidence support the core problem statement
- The document has been reviewed and acknowledged by the initiative lead and product owner
