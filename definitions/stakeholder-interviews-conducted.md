# Stakeholder Interviews Conducted

## What this means

The initiative lead and product counterparts have held structured conversations with key stakeholders — internal users, business partners, domain experts, and affected teams — to gather requirements, surface constraints, and validate assumptions before design work begins.

## Why it matters

Requirements that emerge from documentation alone frequently miss implicit constraints, organizational priorities, and nuanced user expectations. Direct stakeholder interviews surface tacit knowledge early, build alignment across teams, and reduce the risk of delivering technically correct but organizationally misaligned outcomes.

## How to complete this

- Identify all stakeholder groups: end users, business owners, dependent teams, compliance, and operations
- Prepare a structured interview guide covering goals, pain points, constraints, and success criteria
- Conduct interviews with representatives from each stakeholder group, documenting key quotes and themes
- Synthesize interview notes into consolidated findings, grouped by theme
- Share a summary of insights with the broader initiative team and validate key conclusions

## Definition of done

- At least three distinct stakeholder groups have been interviewed
- Interview notes are documented and stored in the initiative's knowledge base
- A synthesized findings summary has been created and shared with the team
- Key constraints and priorities surfaced in interviews are reflected in the requirements documentation
