# Team Capacity Assessed

## What this means
Team capacity assessment establishes how much engineering capacity is actually available for this initiative — accounting for existing commitments, planned time off, on-call rotations, and the overhead of coordination, code review, and meetings. It produces a realistic capacity number to compare against the project scope estimate.

## Why it matters
Estimates made without understanding available capacity are guesses that consistently result in slippage. Teams that commit to more work than they have capacity for create a culture of chronic lateness, burnout, and lowered morale. Honest capacity assessment enables honest conversations about scope before commitments are made.

## How to complete this
- List all engineers contributing to this initiative and their available percentage (accounting for other commitments)
- Identify planned time off, company holidays, and on-call rotations that reduce effective capacity
- Subtract overhead: code review, meetings, planning, and mentoring typically consume 20-40% of engineering time
- Calculate total effective engineer-weeks available for implementation
- Compare available capacity to scope estimate and identify any gap
- Use the gap to drive scope prioritization or timeline negotiation — don't just hope it works out

## Definition of done
- [ ] Available engineering capacity calculated for the project duration
- [ ] Time off, holidays, and on-call load accounted for
- [ ] Overhead estimated and subtracted from gross capacity
- [ ] Capacity vs. scope gap identified and addressed
