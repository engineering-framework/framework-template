# External Resource Needs Assessed

## What this means
External resource assessment evaluates whether this initiative requires resources outside the current team — contractors, consultants, staff augmentation, specialized agencies, or outsourced components. It defines what's needed, when, and how the engagement will be structured.

## Why it matters
Engaging external resources takes time: identifying candidates, procurement processes, contract negotiations, and onboarding can take weeks to months. Teams that discover mid-project that they need external help face the choice of delaying or rushing a procurement process. Assessing needs early creates runway to engage the right resources at the right time.

## How to complete this
- Identify skills or capacity gaps that cannot be filled internally within the required timeline
- Determine the engagement type: full-time contractor, part-time specialist, fixed-scope project, or managed service
- Define the expected duration, start date, and skills required for each external engagement
- Initiate procurement processes early — don't wait until the gap is blocking work
- Plan for knowledge transfer: external resources should not create permanent dependencies

## Definition of done
- [ ] External resource needs identified
- [ ] Engagement type and duration defined for each need
- [ ] Procurement process initiated with sufficient lead time
- [ ] Knowledge transfer plan included in the engagement scope
